Task Instructions

Thanks for participating in this HIT! In this task, you will evaluate an inference derived from a part of a video. Each video is divided into three parts Part 1, Part 2 and Part 3, described below.

  • Part 1: The beginning or setup of the scene in the video.
  • Part 2: The interesting event in the video.
  • Part 3: The result or outcome of the event in the video.
In this task, you will watch only Part 1 of the video, and Part 2 and Part 3 will be hidden. You need to evaluate a description that answers the question, "What could have happened in Part 2 (the middle part) after seeing Part 1?" based on the criteria listed below. Please refer to the example to understand the scoring better.

Criteria:

  1. Correctness (5 Point Scale) Check whether the answer correctly describes what could have happened in part 2 based on part 1.
  2. Depth and Thoughtfulness (5 Point Scale) How much depth in reasoning is covered in the description? Is there sufficient reasoning to support the description?
  3. Level of Detail (5 Point Scale) Does the description have sufficient level of detail to easily identify and differentiate between people/objects and actions?
  4. Grammar (Yes/No) Are there any major grammatical errors or issues?
  5. Visual Contradiction (Yes/No) Does the description follow the content in the video, or contradict the contents of the video? A contradiction happens when the description is impossible, given the video.
